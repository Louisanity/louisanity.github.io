I study photonic quantum processors as a route toward scalable and room-temperature quantum information processing. My work links programmable linear optics, photon counting, tensor-network mappings, and distributed quantum neural-network training.

Current themes include:

- photonic QNNs for parameter-efficient learning and quantum-enhanced knowledge distillation;
- multi-QPU photonic learning workflows with deployable classical inference;
- distributed photonic quantum computing architectures for learning over quantum networks;
- hardware-aware evaluation of qumode capacity, sampling latency, optical-loop structure, and quantum–classical orchestration overhead.

Selected outputs: <em>Nature Photonics</em> 2026, IEEE QCE 2025, Imperial QuEST seed-funded projects.
